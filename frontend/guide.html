How to use ApexBot without gambling blindly

Start with demo mode, understand the contract type, test the strategy, and only consider real trading after you understand the risk and limits.

1. Start with the public demo

Open the demo dashboard first. The demo uses simulated balance and positions, while market data and scanner analysis are clearly labeled. Do not connect real trading until you understand what each button does.

Open demoCheck dashboard, scanner, and charts without login.
Pick a templateUse a starter bot idea instead of building from nothing.
Set limitsStake, daily loss, stop loss, and max trades come before entries.

2. Sign up and verify your email

Create an account with email and password, then click the verification link. Google sign-in is for normal users only; admin access should stay controlled by backend rules and owner permissions.

  • Use a strong password.
  • Do not reuse your Deriv password.
  • Do not share verification links or one-time codes.

3. Telegram is optional

Telegram can help with alerts, community updates, and support, but it should not block the dashboard. If the public Telegram links are not configured yet, you can still use the demo, guide, scanner, and account dashboard.

4. Digits: Over/Under, Even/Odd, Matches/Differs

Digit contracts are simple to understand but easy to misuse. A streak does not guarantee the next result. Use digit templates only for demo learning and always set strict loss limits.

  1. Select a digit market and contract type.
  2. Define the exact rule that creates a signal.
  3. Backtest several market conditions, not only one good period.
  4. Stop after your daily loss limit is reached.

5. Volatility indices: R_10 to R_100

Volatility markets can move quickly. Use the scanner to check trend, volatility, confidence, support, resistance, and invalidation. A good setup should explain why you enter and when the idea is invalid.

  • Trend: avoid trading against strong movement unless the strategy is designed for reversal.
  • Volatility: reduce stake or skip when risk is high.
  • Invalidation: decide what proves the trade idea wrong before entering.

6. Boom and Crash need extra caution

Boom/Crash markets can spike aggressively. A bot that looks calm in demo can still fail when conditions change. Use filters, avoid oversized stakes, and never rely on a single indicator.

  • Use spike-aware rules and volatility checks.
  • Do not increase stake after every loss unless you fully understand the drawdown risk.
  • Prefer small demo tests across multiple sessions over one lucky run.

7. Importing XML or JSON bots

ApexBot can import Deriv XML bots and JSON strategy files, but unknown files should be treated carefully. Validate the file, inspect the rules, and run in demo first.

  1. Import the file in the bot area or visual builder.
  2. Check contract type, stake, duration, stop loss, and restart rules.
  3. Run a demo backtest before saving it as a usable strategy.
  4. Do not run files from strangers in real mode without inspection.

8. Upgrade only for tools, not promises

Paid plans unlock more scanner, copy trading, bot import, marketplace, and real trading features. A subscription does not reduce market risk and does not guarantee profit. Payment status should activate only after backend verification.

Risk rule

Trading involves real financial risk. The AI can show confidence and warnings, but it cannot know the future. Never trade borrowed money, school fees, rent, emergency funds, or money you cannot afford to lose.
